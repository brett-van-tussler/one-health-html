Circulating Strains in iGAS Genomes by Quarter


Over 100 strains of GAS, known as emm-types, exist and can be identified through genomic sequencing. These emm-types are distinguished by a region of the M protein gene, which encodes the cell surface virulence protein.

emm-type Description
emm1 Most common emm-type, responsible for many infections and outbreaks worldwide. Endemic in Arizona, can lead to severe bacterial infections.
emm4 Worldwide distribution, associated with erythromycin resistance. Endemic within Arizona.
emm12 Global prevalence, associated with invasive disease, and reported to be resistant to antibiotics.
emm28 One of the most common emm-types to cause pharyngitis and invasive disease in North America.
emm43 Worldwide distribution, associated with IV drug use, reported resistance to beta-lactam drugs.
emm49 Associated with severe and fatal outbreaks globally, represented ~25% of all emm-types identified in Arizona in 2019-2021
emm59 Hypervirulent strain first identified in Canada in 2006, and responsible for 2015 outbreak in Northern Arizona amongst incarcerated Native American. Associated with severe cases of bacteremia and soft tissue infections, homelessness, and IV drug use.
emm82 Worldwide distribution, associated with IV drug use, endemic strain in Arizona
emm83 Associated with homeless populations, IV drug use, and soft tissue infections
emm89 Associated with global outbreaks, toxic shock syndrome, and endocarditis.
emm91 Global distribution, but sporadically isolated, associated with skin colonization and infection.
emm92 Endemic globally and more common strain among children, reported resistance to macrolide antibiotics.
