Description: The “SARS-CoV-2 Surveillance and Biosecurity Assessment in Wildlife Rehabilitation Facilities” is a new study being conducted with wildlife rehabilitation facilities across the United States. The purpose of this study is to expand our understanding of which North American mammalian wildlife species are susceptible to SARS-CoV-2, and determine the best biosecurity practices for protecting these species and their caretakers. Through this work, we aim to identify species in which documented infection has not previously occurred, and further understand how transmission dynamics of the virus may be influenced by human intervention. This project is a collaboration between TGen North and the University of Florida College of Veterinary Medicine (UF), with support from the U.S. Department of Agriculture Animal and Plant Health Inspection Service (USDA APHIS).
